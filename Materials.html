The AI Footprint

Materials

Semiconductor Production

AI systems rely on advanced semiconductor chips. These chips require highly specialized manufacturing processes that use energy, chemicals, and ultrapure water.

Fabrication facilities are resource-intensive and produce chemical byproducts that must be carefully managed.

Extraction and E-Waste

AI hardware depends on materials such as lithium, cobalt, and rare earth elements. Mining these materials can lead to environmental damage and resource depletion.

As AI technology advances, hardware is replaced more frequently, contributing to growing electronic waste.

Material and Lifecycle Impact

Rare Earth Mining

Extraction processes can cause pollution, habitat disruption, and long-term damage.

Semiconductor Fabrication

Chip production requires energy, chemicals, and generates industrial waste.

Hardware Turnover

Short product lifecycles increase the amount of electronic waste.

Recycling Systems

Efficient recycling can reduce waste, but recovery systems are still limited.
