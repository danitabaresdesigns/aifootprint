The AI Footprint

Energy

Training and Inference

Large AI models require substantial energy during training. This process involves running massive datasets across thousands of processors over long periods of time.

After training, models continue to consume energy during inference. Every time a user submits a prompt or generates output, computational resources are used.

As AI becomes more integrated into everyday tools, this energy demand becomes continuous.

Data Centers and Emissions

AI workloads are concentrated in large-scale data centers that operate continuously. These facilities require stable, high-capacity electricity to function.

Environmental impact depends on infrastructure and how that energy is produced.

Factors Influencing Energy Impact

Grid Carbon Intensity

Emissions depend on the energy source. Fossil fuels increase carbon output.

Hardware Efficiency

More efficient processors reduce the amount of electricity required.

Cooling Design

Inefficient cooling systems increase total energy consumption.

Renewable Energy

Using renewable energy sources lowers environmental impact.
