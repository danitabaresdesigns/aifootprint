Water

Cooling Systems

AI infrastructure generates large amounts of heat. Data centers rely on cooling systems to maintain safe operating temperatures.

Many of these systems use water, especially evaporative cooling, which increases overall water consumption.

Water may also be used indirectly through electricity production that depends on thermal processes.

Regional Impact

Water consumption varies depending on location. Data centers built in drought-prone or water-stressed areas can increase pressure on local resources.

As AI infrastructure expands, water availability becomes an important factor in sustainability.

Water Use Considerations

Cooling Method

Different systems require different amounts of water. Evaporative cooling increases use.

Climate

Hotter climates require more cooling, increasing water demand.

Water Availability

Regions with limited water resources face greater sustainability challenges.

Seasonal Demand

Peak demand periods can strain water supply and infrastructure systems.
